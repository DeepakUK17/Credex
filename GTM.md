# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Strategy

**Word count:** ~500 words

---

## Primary Target User

**Engineering Manager at a 15-50 person Series A startup.**

Specific profile: managing a team of 5-15 developers, each running 2-4 AI subscriptions independently, often expensed without centralized visibility. They feel the budget pressure from their CFO but haven't audited the stack because "it's hard to track individual subscriptions."

**Secondary:** Solo technical founder spending >$300/month on AI tools personally, acutely cost-conscious, knows every dollar.

---

## What They Search For

These are real search queries — not guesses:
- `"cursor vs copilot pricing 2025"`
- `"claude team vs individual plan"`
- `"reduce AI costs for developers"`
- `"chatgpt team plan worth it"`
- `"ai tools budget startup"`
- `"github copilot alternatives cheaper"`

The intent behind each: they already know they might be overpaying. They want external validation and a comparison they don't have to build themselves.

---

## Where They Hang Out Online

1. **Hacker News** — specifically the monthly "Ask HN: What's your AI tool stack?" threads
2. **r/SaaS and r/startups** — founders discussing cost optimization
3. **Indie Hackers** — builders who care deeply about unit economics
4. **X (Twitter)** — follow lists of startup CTOs; #buildinpublic; Cursor and Copilot announcement threads
5. **Linear, Notion, GitHub** — indirect: they live in their tools, not general social media
6. **Slack communities** — "Ramen Club", "SaaStr Community", specific dev-focused Slacks

---

## 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1:**
- Post "Show HN: I built a free AI spend auditor for engineering teams" with a link to the live tool. Include a real example: "I audited my own stack and found $180/month in savings in 60 seconds."
- DM 20 founders/EMs on X who have tweeted about Cursor, Copilot, or Claude in the last 30 days. Personalized message: "Hey [name] — saw you use [tool]. I built a free AI spend auditor that found $X for a team your size. Would love your 2-min feedback."
- Post in Indie Hackers "What I Built This Week" thread.

**Week 2:**
- Post in 3 relevant Slack communities (Ramen Club, etc.) — share the tool with framing around "saving 30-40% on your AI tools."
- Respond to every "AI tool costs" thread on r/startups and r/SaaS with a link to the audit.
- Ask every early user for a testimonial or Twitter post.

**Week 3-4:**
- Publish a short "benchmarks" post: "What engineering teams actually pay for AI tools in 2026" — use aggregate, anonymized data from audits. Post on HN, Indie Hackers.

**Unfair Channel:**
Credex's existing customer base. Every company that has ever used Credex credits is a warm lead. One email to the existing list = 50+ users with intent.
